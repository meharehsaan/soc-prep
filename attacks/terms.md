## 🛡️ SOC & Network Security – Important Terms (Interview Cheat Sheet)

This is a **high-value glossary** of commonly used SOC / Cybersecurity / Network Security terms with **simple interview-ready definitions**.

---

## 🧠 1. Core SOC Terms

#### 🔹 Artifact
**Definition:**
Any evidence left behind after a cyber event or attack.

**Examples:**
- Log files
- Malware files
- Registry changes
- Suspicious processes

---

#### 🔹 Asset
**Definition:**
Any valuable resource in an organization that needs protection.

**Examples:**
- Servers
- Endpoints (laptops, PCs)
- Databases
- Applications

---

#### 🔹 Identity
**Definition:**
A digital representation of a user or system in a network.

**Examples:**
- Username
- Email account
- Service account
- Admin account

---

#### 🔹 Indicator of Compromise (IOC)
**Definition:**
Evidence that a system has been compromised.

**Examples:**
- Malicious IP
- File hash
- Suspicious domain

---

#### 🔹 Indicator of Attack (IOA)
**Definition:**
Behavior showing an ongoing attack attempt.

**Examples:**
- Lateral movement
- Privilege escalation
- Brute force patterns

---

## 🔐 2. Network Security Terms

#### 🔹 Firewall
**Definition:**
A security system that controls incoming and outgoing network traffic based on rules.

---

#### 🔹 IDS (Intrusion Detection System)
**Definition:**
Monitors network traffic and alerts on suspicious activity.

---

#### 🔹 IPS (Intrusion Prevention System)
**Definition:**
Detects and actively blocks malicious traffic.

---

#### 🔹 VPN (Virtual Private Network)
**Definition:**
Encrypted connection over the internet for secure communication.

---

#### 🔹 Proxy
**Definition:**
An intermediary server between user and internet for filtering and privacy.

---

#### 🔹 DNS (Domain Name System)
**Definition:**
Translates domain names into IP addresses.

---

#### 🔹 NAT (Network Address Translation)
**Definition:**
Maps private IP addresses to public IP addresses.

---

## 🧩 3. SOC Operations Terms

#### 🔹 SIEM (Security Information and Event Management)
**Definition:**
A system that collects, analyzes, and correlates security logs.

Examples:
- Splunk
- Microsoft Sentinel

---

#### 🔹 EDR (Endpoint Detection and Response)
**Definition:**
Tool that monitors endpoint behavior for threats.

---

#### 🔹 XDR (Extended Detection and Response)
**Definition:**
Unified security platform combining endpoint, network, email, and cloud detection.

---

#### 🔹 SOAR (Security Orchestration, Automation, and Response)
**Definition:**
Automates incident response workflows in SOC.

---

#### 🔹 Triage
**Definition:**
Process of prioritizing security alerts based on severity and risk.

---

## 🧪 4. Threat & Attack Terms

#### 🔹 Malware
**Definition:**
Malicious software designed to harm or exploit systems.

Types:
- Virus
- Trojan
- Ransomware
- Worm

---

#### 🔹 Phishing
**Definition:**
Social engineering attack to steal credentials via fake emails or websites.

---

#### 🔹 Ransomware
**Definition:**
Malware that encrypts files and demands payment.

---

#### 🔹 Lateral Movement
**Definition:**
When attacker moves across systems inside a network after initial access.

---

#### 🔹 Privilege Escalation
**Definition:**
Gaining higher access rights than originally assigned.

---

## 🌐 5. Network Traffic & Logs Terms

#### 🔹 Log
**Definition:**
Recorded activity of system, application, or network events.

---

#### 🔹 Packet
**Definition:**
Small unit of data transmitted over a network.

---

#### 🔹 Flow
**Definition:**
Collection of packets sharing same source, destination, and protocol.

---

#### 🔹 Beaconing
**Definition:**
Periodic communication from infected system to attacker (C2 server).

---

## 🔎 6. Detection & Analysis Terms

#### 🔹 False Positive
**Definition:**
Benign activity incorrectly flagged as malicious.

---

#### 🔹 False Negative
**Definition:**
Malicious activity that is not detected.

---

#### 🔹 Correlation
**Definition:**
Linking multiple security events to identify an attack pattern.

---

#### 🔹 Baseline
**Definition:**
Normal behavior pattern of users or systems used for anomaly detection.

---

#### 🔹 Anomaly
**Definition:**
Deviation from normal behavior.

---

## 🧱 7. Infrastructure Terms

#### 🔹 Endpoint
**Definition:**
Any device connected to a network (laptop, server, mobile).

---

#### 🔹 Server
**Definition:**
System that provides services to other devices.

---

#### 🔹 Cloud Security
**Definition:**
Protection of cloud-based infrastructure and services.

---

#### 🔹 Active Directory (AD)
**Definition:**
Centralized system for managing users, permissions, and authentication in Windows environments.

---

## 🧠 8. Threat Intelligence Terms

#### 🔹 Threat Intelligence
**Definition:**
Information about current or potential cyber threats.

---

#### 🔹 APT (Advanced Persistent Threat)
**Definition:**
Highly skilled attacker who maintains long-term access in a network.

---

#### 🔹 TTPs (Tactics, Techniques, Procedures)
**Definition:**
Behavior patterns used by attackers.

---

#### 🔹 IOC Feed
**Definition:**
Stream of indicators used for detection and blocking threats.
